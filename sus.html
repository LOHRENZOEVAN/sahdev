Sustainable Agriculture

Enhancing food security and agricultural value chains

Our Approach to Sustainable Agriculture

At SAHDEV, we believe that sustainable agriculture is the foundation of community resilience and food security. Our comprehensive approach integrates traditional knowledge with modern techniques to create farming systems that are productive, profitable, and environmentally sound.

Initiatives in Promoting School Farms SAHDEV works with primary and secondary schools to promote school farms, aiming to increase knowledge on agriculture among young minds, enhance school feeding for better nutrition, increase school enrollment, and generate revenue for other school activities. This initiative is part of a broader effort to integrate agricultural education into school curricula and improve overall educational outcomes.

Crop & Livestock Production

We work with farmers to improve their crop and livestock production through:

  • Introduction of high-yielding, climate-resilient crop varieties
  • Training on improved agricultural practices and technologies
  • Support for sustainable livestock management and animal health
  • Development of irrigation systems for year-round production
  • Promotion of organic farming and reduced chemical inputs

Value Chain Development

We understand that production is just one part of a successful agricultural system. That's why we focus on the entire value chain:

  • Creating market linkages between farmers and buyers
  • Improving post-harvest handling and storage infrastructure
  • Supporting local processing to add value to raw agricultural products
  • Organizing farmers into cooperatives for better bargaining power
  • Building business skills and entrepreneurship capacity

Success Stories

Maize Productivity Project

In 2023, our intervention with 500 smallholder farmers in the North region resulted in a 60% increase in maize yields through improved varieties and training on good agricultural practices. Farmers reported an average income increase of 45%.

Women's Vegetable Cooperative

We supported a women's cooperative of 75 members to establish irrigated vegetable gardens, providing year-round production and income. The cooperative now supplies fresh vegetables to local markets and restaurants, increasing household nutrition and creating sustainable livelihoods.
