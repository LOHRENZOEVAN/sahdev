Environmental Conservation

Protecting biodiversity and promoting climate-smart solutions

Our Environmental Initiatives

At SAHDEV, we understand that environmental conservation is essential for sustainable development. Our programs integrate ecological principles with community needs to create solutions that protect natural resources while supporting livelihoods.

Biodiversity Conservation

We work to protect and restore the rich biodiversity of our regions through:

  • Community-based forest management and restoration
  • Protected area support and management
  • Wildlife conservation initiatives
  • Native species propagation and planting
  • Indigenous knowledge preservation and application

Climate-Smart Practices

We promote adaptation and mitigation strategies to address climate change:

  • Climate-resilient agricultural techniques
  • Renewable energy adoption (solar, biogas)
  • Waste management and recycling programs
  • Water conservation and watershed protection
  • Sustainable alternatives to firewood and charcoal

Environmental Education

We build ecological awareness and stewardship through:

  • School-based environmental clubs and curriculum
  • Community awareness campaigns and training
  • Environmental advocacy and policy engagement
  • Eco-tourism and nature connection programs

Success Stories

Community Forest Restoration

Working with five villages in the Western Region, we've restored 350 hectares of degraded forest land through community-led efforts. Over 100,000 indigenous trees have been planted, creating habitat for wildlife and improving watershed protection.

Clean Energy Initiative

Our program has introduced fuel-efficient cookstoves to 1,200 households and installed solar systems in 15 villages, reducing firewood consumption by 60% and providing clean energy access to over 5,000 people.
